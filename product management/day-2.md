# Day 2: Problem Discovery and User Research

## Core Message
Effective product management starts with understanding the problems your product aims to solve. Problem discovery involves identifying pain points and needs within your target audience, while user research provides the insights needed to shape a solution that aligns with those needs.

## Key Concepts
1. **Problem Discovery**: 
   - Identifying and defining the primary problems your product will solve. This process helps ensure the product is relevant and valuable.
   
2. **User Research**:
   - A systematic approach to understanding user behaviors, needs, and motivations through various research methods.
   - Methods include:
     - **User Interviews**: Conversations with potential users to gain qualitative insights.
     - **Surveys**: Structured questionnaires to gather data from a larger audience.
     - **Competitive Analysis**: Reviewing competitors to understand market gaps and opportunities.

3. **Personas**:
   - Fictional characters that represent the different user types within a targeted demographic, based on real user data.

## Suggested Activity
1. **Identify Key Problems**:
   - Make a list of potential problems your product will address. Consider who will benefit from solving these issues and how they will benefit.

2. **Conduct Basic User Research**:
   - If possible, conduct short user interviews with people who fit your target audience. Alternatively, create a simple survey with questions that will help uncover pain points related to your product.

3. **Build a Persona**:
   - Create a basic persona based on your research. Include attributes like demographic info, goals, challenges, and motivations.

4. **Competitor Research**:
   - Select two competitors and write down their strengths, weaknesses, and how they address similar problems.
